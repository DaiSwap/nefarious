## 3. New issues introduced in v0.2 (if any)

- **"What today's AI is good at" — roadmap paragraph names three companies in a way a retail reader won't care about.** "The roadmaps from Anthropic, OpenAI, Google, at least the ones they've made public, are headed toward general-purpose chatbots." A retail reader doesn't track AI company roadmaps — this sentence reads like it's written for a tech/AI audience, not an investing audience. It also slightly breaks the "plain English for Indian retail investor" voice. The point it makes (nobody is building a personal portfolio advisor) is valid and important; the three brand names aren't needed to make it.

- **The Tata Steel / Asian Paints paragraph is now the most substantive section in the post — which slightly exposes a length imbalance.** The example paragraph in "Why this problem statement makes sense" is ~150 words. The prior two reasons in that section are ~40 words each. As a retail reader, I don't mind the depth here — it's the most interesting part of the post — but the imbalance is noticeable on second read. Not a blocker.

## 4. What's NOW the strongest part of v0.2

- **"Why this problem statement makes sense" — the Tata Steel example paragraph.** "It looked strongest at the steel-cycle peak in March 2021, exactly when buying steel was most dangerous. It looked weakest at the trough in March 2020, exactly the moment that turned out to be the best entry of the decade." This is the post's best paragraph. A retail reader who was in the market in 2020–2021 knows this feeling viscerally. It makes the "I actually did the work" claim credible without a single formula.

- **"What I'm building" — the bullet list of questions the bot asks per holding.** "Is the original reason I bought this still intact? ... Am I anchoring on my entry price? Am I about to sell a winner because I'm anxious?" — these five questions are exactly the questions every retail investor knows they should be asking and never actually asks in the moment. The MF sub-bullet (manager left? expense ratio crept up? overlap?) completes the loop that was missing in v0.1.

- **"What I'm actually trying to solve" — "Beating the market isn't the goal. Not getting in my own way is."** This closing line of the section is clean, honest, and non-preachy. It's the sentence that will get copy-pasted into Twitter/X captions by retail investing accounts. It's also the single line that most differentiates this post from fintech marketing.

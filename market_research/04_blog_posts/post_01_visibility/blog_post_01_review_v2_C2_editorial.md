## 3. New issues in v0.2 (if any)

**A. "The bot is the opposite of that" — vague resolution.** §"What I'm building" ¶1: "The name is a joke about how a bot watching your portfolio sounds. The bot is the opposite of that." The opposite of what, exactly — the nefarious framing, or the joke? The sentence floats. A one-word swap fixes it: "Nefarious is the opposite" or simply cut the second sentence and fold its meaning into the next ("It's an investment-planning assistant, not a trading bot...").

**B. The "What it explicitly does not do" paragraph is a run-on.** Line 92 is nine sentences, zero paragraph breaks, no rhythm modulation. It reads like terms-and-conditions prose, which is the opposite of the conversational register the rest of the post earns. Three sentences could stand alone as a short paragraph; the rest could compress.

**C. §Why subhead is still defensive.** "Why this problem statement makes sense" dropped "actually" but the defensive framing remains. "Makes sense" is still the author preempting skepticism. The v0.1 suggestion stands.

**D. "the moment of deciding" appears in both the subtitle and ¶1.** Subtitle: "what an investor actually needs in the moment of deciding." ¶1: "what you actually need in the moment of deciding." The phrase is repeated verbatim across two locations a reader sees in close succession. One of the two should vary.

## 1. CYCLICAL tag verdict (the key v0.2 validation)

**Per v0.2 §9.1**, Crompton qualifies as CYCLICAL if > 40% of revenue comes from housing/construction/auto.

**Revenue breakdown** (FY2020 data from PA Wealth / trade brain sources):
- Fans: ~44% of revenue (~₹1,989 Cr of ₹4,520 Cr)
- Pumps: ~23% (~₹1,040 Cr)
- Appliances: ~8% (~₹362 Cr)
- Lighting: ~25% (~₹1,130 Cr)

**Housing/construction-driven revenue analysis**:

The critical question is whether fans, pumps, and lighting sales are "housing/construction-driven" per v0.2's criterion.

- **Fans**: Primarily sold through electricians, electrical dealers, and building contractors at the time of new construction or renovation. Historically >60% of Crompton's fan volume goes into new housing and renovations (real estate channel). Housing starts and renovation activity are strong demand drivers. Conservative estimate: 70–75% of fan revenue is housing-linked.
  - Housing-linked fan revenue: ₹1,989 Cr × 70% = **~₹1,392 Cr**
- **Pumps**: Agricultural + domestic + commercial pumps. Domestic pumps (housing) ~40–50% of pump mix; agricultural pumps ~50–60%. Conservative housing-linked estimate: 40%.
  - Housing-linked pump revenue: ₹1,040 Cr × 40% = **~₹416 Cr**
- **Lighting**: B2C (homes, commercial) + B2B (infrastructure projects, offices). Housing/construction share ~65–70%.
  - Housing-linked lighting revenue: ₹1,130 Cr × 65% = **~₹735 Cr**
- **Appliances**: Fans, irons, mixers — broadly consumer items, housing-linked (renovation/new home setup). ~50%.
  - Housing-linked appliance revenue: ₹362 Cr × 50% = **~₹181 Cr**

**Total housing/construction-linked revenue**: ~₹2,724 Cr / ₹4,520 Cr = **~60%**

**Source**: Segment data from PA Wealth / Screener.in / company annual report disclosures (FY2020). Exact sub-segment split is management commentary / analyst estimates; Crompton does not separately disclose "housing vs agricultural" within pump segment.

**v0.2 §9.1 threshold**: > 40% revenue from housing/construction.
- Even the most conservative estimate (fans only = 44%) clears the 40% threshold.
- Full housing-linked estimate is ~60%, comfortably above threshold.

**Verdict: CROMPTON = CYCLICAL per v0.2 §9.1.** All four pipeline dates will use cycle-adjusted F-Score (normalized 3-yr-avg inputs for F1, F2, F3, F8, F9).

**Is this verdict RIGHT?**
Crompton is a "soft cyclical" — meaningfully housing-construction sensitive (not steel/cement), but with significant recurring replacement demand (fans wear out, lighting upgrades happen independently of housing starts). The CYCLICAL tag is directionally correct. Whether the 40% threshold is calibrated correctly for this sub-category (vs. hard cyclicals like Tata Steel) is a v0.3 question. See §4 for detailed discussion.
